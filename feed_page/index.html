Gene therapy involves introducing, repairing, or replacing genes to treat or prevent diseases. Researchers have made significant progress in using gene therapy to address various genetic disorders, such as certain types of inherited blindness, hemophilia, and muscular dystrophy.

Don Smith
Nanotechnology is being used in cancer treatment

Nanotechnology has shown promise in revolutionizing cancer treatment by providing targeted and more effective therapeutic approaches. Targeted Therapy: Nanoparticles can be designed to carry anticancer drugs directly to cancer cells, minimizing damage to healthy tissues. This targeted drug delivery can enhance the effectiveness of treatment while reducing side effects. Enhanced Permeability and Retention (EPR) Effect: Nanoparticles can take advantage of the EPR effect, which is the tendency of abnormal blood vessels around tumors to be leaky. This allows nanoparticles to accumulate preferentially in the tumor tissue.
